Your typical home or office PC has been able to find its way on an uncharted network since there were "home" PCs with networking. However, since the late 90s, there's been yet more untapped power in that wayfinding. Through a combination of Intel's cost-reduction dreams, and some tricks borrowed from the workstations of the 80s, join us in Press[ing] F12 to boot from the network. Along the way we'll explore some truly arcane protocols, and jargon that conflicts in just the wrong ways.
